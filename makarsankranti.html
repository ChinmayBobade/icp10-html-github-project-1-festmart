Makarsankranti

 

Makar Sankranti is the celebration of the sun's journey from the Southern to the Northern Hemisphere and is considered an auspicious time. Makar translates as 'Capricorn' and Sankranti means 'transition'. The astrological configuration on Makar Sankranti is called 'Maha-snana-yoga.

 When it's celebrated Makar Sankranti is celebrated annually on January 14, or January 15 in a leap year. What it means Makar Sankranti is a time to give thanks to nature and Mother Earth, and to mark the beginning of a new cycle. It's also a harvest festival that marks the end of winter and the start of the harvesting season. How it's celebrated Celebrations include: Bathing: Some people bathe in rivers and lakes, especially at the confluence of holy rivers. Giving gifts: People give each other gifts, especially til, a traditional sweet made from sesame. Feasting: People celebrate with a feast that includes a dish made of green gram, rice, and jaggery. Flying kites: Some believe that flying kites awakens the gods who have been resting during winter.
